Canada

The Canadian Security Intelligence Service (CSIS) is Canada's national intelligence agency, responsible for safeguarding the country's national security. CSIS investigates potential threats such as terrorism, espionage, foreign interference, sabotage, and subversion. It gathers intelligence through various methods, including human sources and signals intelligence, operating both domestically and internationally. CSIS also conducts security assessments for individuals seeking access to sensitive government positions. The agency provides intelligence reports and advice to the Canadian government to inform national security policies and decisions. CSIS operates under the Canadian Security Intelligence Service Act and is subject to oversight by bodies like the National Security and Intelligence Committee of Parliamentarians (NSICOP) and the National Security and Intelligence Review Agency (NSIRA) to ensure it operates lawfully and respects the rights of Canadians. In essence, CSIS functions as Canada's primary intelligence collector and analyst, dedicated to protecting the nation from diverse security threats.

Daniel Rogers: Director of the CSIS
Daniel Rogers is a seasoned national security expert with over 20 years of experience. Since May 2023, he has served as Deputy National Security and Intelligence Advisor (NSIA) to the Prime Minister, aiding in national security coordination and advice. Concurrently, he is the Deputy Secretary to the Cabinet (Emergency Preparedness), supporting federal emergency readiness and response. Before these roles, Rogers was the Associate Chief of the Communications Security Establishment (CSE) from January 2022. His extensive career at CSE began in 2002, where he progressed through leadership roles in intelligence programs and oversaw policy compliance as the Director of Signals Intelligence (SIGINT) Requirements.
