Germany

The Federal Intelligence Service (Bundesnachrichtendienst, BND) is Germany’s foreign intelligence agency, operating under the direct authority of the Chancellor’s Office and headquartered in central Berlin. Established during the Cold War in 1956 as the successor to the covert Gehlen Organization, the BND quickly became one of the world’s largest and most influential intelligence agencies, second only to the CIA at its peak. With a workforce of around 6,500 employees stationed at over 300 domestic and international locations, the BND serves as an early warning system for threats to German interests abroad. The agency specializes in wiretapping, electronic surveillance, and gathering both civil and military intelligence on issues ranging from terrorism and proliferation of weapons of mass destruction to organized crime and information warfare. Closely cooperating with Germany’s domestic security services and military intelligence, the BND remains a central pillar in securing the nation’s security and strategic interests worldwide, with a longstanding focus on Russia, the Middle East, and global non-state threats.

Born in Essen, Germany in 1962, the current President of the Bundesnachrichtendienst (BND), Germany’s foreign intelligence agency, brings a distinguished background in law, military service, and high-level government administration. After completing his Abitur, he served in the field intelligence corps, reaching the rank of colonel in the reserves. He then pursued law studies in Bonn and Lausanne, graduating with both the first and second state legal examinations. His career in public service began at the Federal Chancellery in 1995, followed by roles as an expert consultant in the Bundestag and leadership positions within the Federal Ministry of the Interior and the Federal Ministry of Finance. Earning a doctorate in law in 2008, he managed significant portfolios including privatisations and federal assets. Since 2016, he has led the BND, leveraging his legal expertise, political acumen, and security background to oversee Germany’s global intelligence operations. He is widowed and the father of two children.
